Unsolicited Airdrop

Opening 8 June 2017
Broad Art Center EDA
Gallery Hours
Mon-Fri 10am-5pm

The contemporary digital landscape is awash with solicitations of various kinds vying ceaselessly for eyeballs and fingertips. As the twenty first century whirls onward, many have found it difficult to stake a **no soliciting** sign down into the bedrock of clickbait journalism and hyperlinked comments that make up the front yards of their Instagram feeds. How much of this uninvited visual information slips past our conscious awareness and into our background streams? Who is controlling it, and what do they want? In any case, the option to press ‘x’ in the corner of a web advertisement or the choice of whether or not to accept an airdropped file that is already shown on screen is a bit like being asked, “Can I ask you a question?” There is not much choice at all.

The artists and designers of Unsolicited Airdrop take pride in engaging within this space of visual combat. We invite you to look critically and think skeptically about this presented body of artworks as airdrops. Furthermore, much like the contents of an airdrop, which often include items ranging from smart bombs to humanitarian aid, transmission of content to specific places and locations is of the utmost importance. This show is being presented in a specific place at a specific time, and the artists would like to recognize and respect this fact. To encourage engagement with bodies in physical space and specific instances, certain exhibition materials will only be made available via airdrop to those who attend the show in person.
